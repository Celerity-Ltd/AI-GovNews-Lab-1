Step 2 — Add the knowledge base

A knowledge base lets the agent look things up in your documents and cite them as the source of its answers. We're using Riverside Borough Council's full council tax handbook — bands, discounts, payment methods, moving home, enforcement, FAQs.

You'll select this knowledge in the steps below. If you want the source: council_tax_knowledge.pdf
Download

Step 2.1 — Select the existing knowledge

The workshop tenant has the council tax knowledge base pre-loaded under Existing Knowledge. In production you'd upload your own documents or connect to an external store (database, SharePoint, etc.).

  1. On your agent, scroll down to Knowledge.
  2. Click Add Source.
  3. Choose Existing Knowledge.
  4. Select Council Tax Knowledge from the list.
  5. Confirm. The knowledge appears in your agent's Knowledge section.
Why a knowledge base matters: the agent's answers about bands, discounts and procedures will come directly from this document, with a citation. Citizens (and Amir) can click the citation to see exactly where the answer came from.

Step 2.2 — Quick test (optional)

You can try the knowledge base straight away if you want a sanity check. Paste this into the playground chat:

Try this in the playground
What are the council tax bands and their annual charges?

You should see Riverside Borough Council's specific band information come back. Click the citation dropdown at the end of the message to see exactly which part of the document the answer came from.
